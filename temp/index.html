About ROCCON

ROCCON (Realms of Cyber conference) is an expansion on our previous, wildly successful, Autohackers conference. We are a think-tank, conference, and hacker meet with a focus on the physical domains. We see a future where land, air, sea, space, cognitive, and biotechnological industries are well supported to make the Australian digital world safer. Our vision is simple: to help everyone grow and protect against online threats. We're here to make sure that cyber professionals and those who need their support can easily connect, creating a strong and united community that keeps our digital spaces secure and thriving.

Speaker Name

Charles O'Connell

CEO, Advisor & Investor
How mis/disinformation affects Australian enterprise.

Charles O'Connell started his career as a diplomat with the UK's Foreign and Commonwealth Office. With over two decades of leadership in the tech and cybersecurity sectors, he has a proven track record of driving growth and fostering innovation. Currently, he is an Advisor at Balmoral Industries and a Co-Founder and Investor at a stealth startup. His past roles include Chief Growth Officer at CyberCX, where he spearheaded major expansion initiatives, and Managing Director at Accenture, focusing on cybersecurity solutions. Charles holds a BA (Hons.) in Politics from The University of Sheffield and a Postgraduate Diploma in Law from BPP Law School.

Michael Creagh, PHD

Michael Creagh, PHD

CEO
Skyborne Technologies
Cerberus MI Kinetic Effects with AI Targeting

The Cerberus MI UAS is a man-packable, lethal 22 kg sUAS. Armed with a 40mm Low velocity grenade launcher, 12 Ga shotgun, 5.7 x 28mm Submachine gun or M72 LAW, it is intended to be used at the Company and platoon level to provide organic, direct aerial fire support. The Cerberus MI supports onboard AI target recognition (by Athena AI) which enables “slew-to-cue” semi-automated targeting. This has been demonstrated in live fire exercises to the USMC, ADF and US Army. The Cerberus MI is a modular payload to enable integration of a variety of kinetic and non-kinetic payloads for operation flexibility. This presentation will address the current state of the Cerberus and its targeting system (including limitations) and cyber resilience strategies employed. Skyborne Technologies is an Australian Defence company developing smart aerial robotics and weapon systems for Defence and Security customers.

Dr. Michael Creagh is the CEO at Skyborne Technologies. He has been working in the Guidance, Navigation and Control (GNC) field since 2007, graduating with a PhD in missile guidance in 2011. He has produced two novel guidance laws for space craft and spinning rockets, an adaptive control law for a hypersonic glider and a plethora of intelligent navigation algorithms. Michael has published on a variety of topics and was highlighted by Popular Science Magazine, ABC Television’s Catalyst and Aviation week for GNC work on the SCRAMSPACE 1 hypersonic flight experiment

Nicholas Lindsay

Nicholas Lindsay

Director & Pilot
MATES Ventures
SpaceX Mindset

Director & Pilot at MATES Ventures, Nicholas draws from his insights into SpaceX's operational strategies to present "The SpaceX Mentality." With an MBA from the Texas McCombs School of Business and a B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from the United States Naval Academy, Nicholas has extensive experience in aerospace leadership and operations management.
